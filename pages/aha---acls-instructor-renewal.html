AHA - ACLS Instructor Renewal

IF YOUR INSTRUCTOR CARD IS CURRENT: To apply for renewal, please complete the items below and email documents at least a week prior to  YOUR EXPIRATION. Email the following items as PDFs to renewal@910cpr.com . Instructor Renewal Checklist. Complete only Sections 1 and 2.  Section 3 and Section 4 will be completed by TCF CURRENT AHA Provider Card Verification of attendance of updates as required within the previous 2 years.  If there were no mandatory updates to attend, you will leave this area blank. Class rosters of any classes completed through secondary alignments.  Instructors are required to teach 4 classes in the 2 year certification period to complete renewal.  If they were all done with Coastal CPR / 910CPR, nothing needs to be submitted here. These 4 classes can be any combination of classroom provider courses and/or Skills testing sessions for e-Learning courses for the disipline you are renewing       An American Heart Association (AHA) Instructor has to renew their certification every two years.  When an AHA instructor needs to recertify, they do not have to go back through an instructor course. They do, however, have other processes to complete to maintain their certification. AHA INSTRUCTOR RENEWAL REQUIREMENTS: Teach a minimum of 4 classes in 2 years These classes can be a mix of Instructor Led or Heartcode courses Pass both the Provider and Instructor exams for each discipline taught Complete Skills Verification for each discipline taught Keep current with all updates from the AHA Be monitored by Training Center Coordinator or an AHA Faculty instructor designated by the TCC The monitoring instructor will fill out the appropriate paperwork to process The instructor is also responsible for maintaining AHA standards as outlined in the ECC Leadership Code of Conduct and the Program Administration Manual (PAM). Failure to meet these requirements will result in the need to take the Instructor Course from the beginning, unalignment from the Training Center, or possible termination of certification.   We, of course, want to help you avoid these frustrations.  See below.
