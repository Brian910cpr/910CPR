AHA - BLS Provider - In-person Initial Instructor-led Classroom for Expired or New BLS

AHA BLS Provider – In-Person – Initial This American Heart Association (AHA) Basic Life Support (BLS) Provider course is designed for healthcare professionals who are taking BLS for the first time or whose certification has expired. It provides foundational skills needed to recognize life-threatening emergencies and respond with high-quality CPR and AED use. Course content is delivered through instructor-led training, hands-on practice, and scenario-based instruction covering adult, child, and infant CPR.   We recommend reviewing the BLS Quick Reference Guide before class. Course Highlights: High-quality CPR for adults, children, and infants Use of AED and relief of choking Effective team dynamics and communication BLS Chain of Survival and emergency response steps What to Expect: This class includes video instruction, instructor guidance, and supervised practice. Instructor-to-student ratio is 1:6 during skills sessions to meet AHA training standards.   Course Length: Approximately 3.5 to 4 hours   Certification: American Heart Association BLS Provider eCard (valid for 2 years), issued upon successful completion of the course requirements.   AHA® is a registered trademark of the American Heart Association and is used with permission. 910CPR is an authorized provider aligned with an AHA Training Center.
